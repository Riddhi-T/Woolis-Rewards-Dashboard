The primary objective of this project was to evaluate the effectiveness of the loyalty program by monitoring key performance indicators (KPIs) such as member registration trends, point redemption behavior, and store-level performance.


Key Metrics and Findings

    1) Scale and Revenue: The program managed 10,000 members (registered in CY23) who completed 106,000 transactions, generating a total revenue of $31.187M AUD.
    
    2) Member Health: The loyalty base is highly engaged, with 90% of members classified as "Active." Only 5% are inactive and 5% are suspended.
    
    3) Points Lifecycle: While 31.1M points were earned, only 772K points have been redeemed, leaving approximately 2.7M points remaining to be utilized by customers.
    
    4) Registration Trends: Analysis showed a significant surge in member registrations between July 2023 and November 2023, indicating successful mid-year marketing or seasonal acquisition campaigns.

Consumer Behavior Insights

    1) Redemption Preferences: Customers overwhelmingly prefer direct financial benefits. 70% of points are redeemed for Shopping Discounts, while 20% are used for Fuel Discounts. High-travel-value rewards like Qantas Points (6%) and charitable Donations (4%) represent a smaller share of the redemption pool.
    
    2) Top Performing Locations: Chatswood, Liverpool, and Manly are the leading locations in terms of points earned.
